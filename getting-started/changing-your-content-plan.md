# Changing Your Content Plan

Your Content Plan controls what AIBC generates every week. It defines the volume, mix, topics, and timing of your content generation.

## What is a Content Plan?

A Content Plan specifies:
- **Content volume**: How many posts/pieces are generated per week
- **Content mix**: The types of content (social posts, blogs, emails, etc.)
- **Weekly topics/pillars**: Main themes for each week
- **Campaign boosts**: Temporary increases around launches or events
- **Platform distribution**: How content is distributed across platforms

## Accessing Your Content Plan

1. Navigate to **Content Hub** in the left sidebar
2. Click the **"Content Plan"** tab at the top
3. You'll see your current plan configuration

## Changing Content Volume

**To adjust how much content is generated:**

1. Go to **Content Hub** → **Content Plan** tab
2. Find **"Weekly Content Volume"** section
3. Adjust the slider or enter a number
4. **Tier limits apply:**
   - **Free**: Up to 10 pieces per week
   - **Pro**: Up to 50 pieces per week
   - **Enterprise**: Unlimited
5. Click **"Save"**

**What counts as a "piece":**
- One social media post
- One blog post
- One email newsletter
- One video script
- One podcast script

**Best practices:**
- Start with a lower volume and increase as you refine your Brand Kit
- Consider your capacity to review and publish
- Balance quality with quantity

## Changing Content Mix

**To adjust the types of content generated:**

1. In **Content Plan**, find **"Content Mix"** section
2. You'll see different content types with toggles or percentages:
   - **Social Posts**: Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Instagram, Facebook
   - **Blog Posts**: WordPress, Medium, or other blog platforms
   - **Email Newsletters**: Mailchimp, ConvertKit, etc.
   - **Video Scripts**: YouTube, TikTok, Instagram Reels
   - **Podcast Scripts**: Audio content
3. **Adjust the mix:**
   - Toggle content types on/off
   - Adjust percentages if using percentage-based distribution
   - Set minimum/maximum for each type
4. Click **"Save"**

**Example mixes:**
- **Social-first**: 70% social posts, 20% blogs, 10% emails
- **Content marketing**: 40% blogs, 30% social, 20% emails, 10% video
- **Video-focused**: 50% video scripts, 30% social, 20% blogs

## Updating Weekly Topics

**To change what topics are covered:**

1. In **Content Plan**, find **"Weekly Topics"** or **"Content Pillars"** section
2. **View current topics:**
   - See topics scheduled for the current week
   - See topics for upcoming weeks
3. **Edit topics:**
   - Click **"Edit Topics"** for a specific week
   - Add new topics
   - Remove topics
   - Reorder topics by priority
4. **Add topic details:**
   - Topic name
   - Description or angle
   - Target audience
   - Key points to cover
5. Click **"Save"**

**Topic best practices:**
- **3-5 topics per week** is optimal
- Mix evergreen and timely topics
- Align with your content pillars
- Consider seasonal relevance
- Balance educational, promotional, and engagement content

## Setting Up Campaign Boosts

Campaign boosts temporarily increase content volume around launches, events, or promotions.

**To set up a campaign boost:**

1. In **Content Plan**, find **"Campaigns"** or **"Boosts"** section
2. Click **"Add Campaign"**
3. **Configure campaign:**
   - **Name**: Campaign name (e.g., "Product Launch - Q1")
   - **Start date**: When boost begins
   - **End date**: When boost ends
   - **Volume increase**: How much more content (e.g., +50%)
   - **Focus topics**: Specific topics for campaign
   - **Platforms**: Which platforms to prioritize
4. Click **"Save"**

**Campaign boost examples:**
- **Product launch**: +100% content for 2 weeks, focus on product features
- **Event promotion**: +50% content for 1 week, focus on event details
- **Seasonal campaign**: +75% content for 1 month, focus on seasonal themes

## Platform Distribution

**To control how content is distributed across platforms:**

1. In **Content Plan**, find **"Platform Distribution"** section
2. **Set distribution:**
   - **Equal distribution**: Same amount to each platform
   - **Custom distribution**: Set percentages per platform
   - **Priority-based**: Prioritize certain platforms
3. **Platform options:**
   - Twitter/X
   - LinkedIn
   - Instagram
   - Facebook
   - YouTube
   - TikTok
   - Blog (WordPress, etc.)
   - Email
4. Click **"Save"**

**Distribution strategies:**
- **Platform-specific**: More content for your strongest platform
- **Balanced**: Equal distribution across all platforms
- **Audience-focused**: More content where your audience is

## Content Generation Schedule

**To set when content is generated:**

1. In **Content Plan**, find **"Generation Schedule"** section
2. **Choose frequency:**
   - **Daily**: Content generated every day
   - **Weekly**: Content generated once per week (default)
   - **Bi-weekly**: Content generated every two weeks
3. **Set generation day:**
   - Which day of the week content is generated
   - Time of day (if applicable)
4. Click **"Save"**

**Schedule considerations:**
- Weekly generation is recommended for most users
- Allows time to review and approve content
- Aligns with typical content planning cycles

## Review and Approval Settings

**To control content review workflow:**

1. In **Content Plan**, find **"Review Settings"** section
2. **Set approval requirements:**
   - **Auto-publish**: Content publishes automatically (requires approval toggle off in Settings)
   - **Require approval**: All content must be approved before publishing
   - **Selective approval**: Only certain types require approval
3. **Set review timeline:**
   - How long content waits for review
   - Auto-approve after X days (if enabled)
4. Click **"Save"**

## Viewing Your Content Plan

**To see your current plan:**

1. Go to **Content Hub** → **Content Plan** tab
2. **View summary:**
   - Total weekly volume
   - Content mix breakdown
   - Current week topics
   - Upcoming topics
   - Active campaigns
3. **View calendar:**
   - See content scheduled for each day
   - See topics assigned to each week
   - See campaign periods

## Changing Plans for Specific Weeks

**To customize a specific week:**

1. In **Content Plan**, find the week you want to customize
2. Click **"Customize Week"** or **"Edit Week"**
3. **Override defaults:**
   - Change volume for that week
   - Change topics
   - Add campaign boost
   - Adjust platform distribution
4. Click **"Save"**

**Use cases:**
- Special events or launches
- Seasonal content adjustments
- Testing new content strategies
- Responding to industry news

## Best Practices

### Start Conservative

- Begin with lower volume
- Test content quality first
- Increase gradually as you refine

### Align with Goals

- Match content mix to your marketing goals
- Balance different content types
- Consider your audience preferences

### Plan Ahead

- Set topics 2-4 weeks in advance
- Schedule campaign boosts early
- Adjust based on performance

### Review Regularly

- Check content quality weekly
- Adjust topics based on performance
- Refine mix based on what works

## Common Issues

### Too Much Content

**Solution:** Reduce weekly volume. Focus on quality over quantity.

### Wrong Content Types

**Solution:** Adjust content mix. Disable types you don't need, increase types you want.

### Off-Topic Content

**Solution:** Update weekly topics. Be more specific about what to cover.

### Content Not Generating

**Solution:** Check that Content Plan is saved, verify you haven't hit tier limits, ensure Brand Kit is configured.

## Next Steps

After setting your Content Plan:

1. **Review generated content** in Content Hub
2. **Adjust topics** based on performance
3. **Set up campaigns** for upcoming launches
4. **Monitor analytics** to optimize your plan
